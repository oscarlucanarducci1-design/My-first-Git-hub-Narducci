Italy

Italy is located in southern Europe, occupying a peninsula that extends into the Mediterranean Sea, resembling a boot. It shares borders with France, Switzerland, Austria, and Slovenia, and includes nearly 800 islands, with Sicily and Sardinia being the largest.

  • Construction: Built between 72 A.D. and 80 A.D. under Emperor Vespasian, using stone and concrete with the labor of tens of thousands of slaves.

  • Historical Use: The Colosseum hosted gladiator battles, wild animal hunts, and public spectacles, making it a central entertainment venue in Ancient Rome.

  • Cultural Significance: Today, it stands as a symbol of Ancient Rome and is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the world. These facts highlight the Colosseum's historical, architectural, and cultural importance.
